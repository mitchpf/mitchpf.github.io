Hydrodynamics of Metachronal Swimming


Metachronal rowing hydrodynamics and system design

My Ph.D. research focused on the fluid dynamics of metachronal swimming, which refers to the coorinated "rowing" of multiple paddles (such as the legs of shrimp or oars used in competitive rowing) rather than "flapping" of fins, flukes, or flippers. By using a "metachronal" coordination, where each paddle moves with a slight delay relative to its neighbors, a swimmer can generate more thrust to increase agility and achieve more rapid acceleration.
