Postdoctoral Research Associate (05/2024–present)

Scientific research on the interactions between aquatic animals and their fluid environments. Investigation into how animals are able to sense and manipulate flows and conversely how changes in background flow and hydrostatic pressure affect biomechanics and performance.

School of Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering (MAE) , Oklahoma State University, Stillwater, OK, USA

Graduate Teaching Associate (08/2020–05/2024)

Head teaching assistant for MAE 3333: Fundamental Fluid Dynamics. Responsibilities included giving occasional lectures and holding exam review sessions, coordinating office hours for 4 teaching assistants, maintaining weekly office hours and administration and grading of homework and exams.

Teaching assistant for MAE 4273/5080: Experimental Fluid Dynamics. Assisted in the development of hands-on laboratory curriculum to introduce students to a variety of data acquisition and analysis techniques used in experimental fluid dynamics. Advised student groups on experimental design choices and data analyses for course projects. Guest lectured on performing data analysis in MATLAB.

Graduate Research Associate [Applied Fluid Mechanics Lab] (08/2018–12/2023)

Participated in a variety of research projects in the field of experimental fluid dynamics including the development and application of robotic models to study crustacean swimming, insect flight, cetacean breathing and human coughing, as well as projects examining particle filtration in aquatic and aerial environments. Served as a research mentor for 13 Undergraduate, 5 Master’s and 2 beginning PhD students across fields of Engineering, Biology, Physics and Education.

Undergraduate Research Assistant [Applied Fluid Mechanics Lab] (08/2016–05/2018)

Assisted on projects involving the role of bristled wings in the fluid dynamics of tiny insect flight. Performed self-guided research to investigate the effect of changing bristle length relative to wing membrane size that resulted in a first-authored journal publication as an undergraduate.
