Bio

I am a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Biology at Providence College. I received my PhD in Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering from Oklahoma State University in May 2024, and now apply my knowledge of engineering and physics to study how animals interact with the physical world around them. My research is focused on discovering how aquatic animals generate hydrodynamic thrust for swimming and how they sense hydrodynamic signals to detect potential predators and prey in their surrounding environment.

My research is primarily conducted at the Marine Biological Laboratory in Woods Hole, MA alongside Professor Jack Costello (Providence College) and Professor Sean Colin (Roger Williams University)
